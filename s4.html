TWIN TOWER

The Petronas Twin Towers, located in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, are the tallest twin towers in the world, standing at 452 meters (1,483 feet) tall. Completed in 1998, the towers were designed by architect César Pelli and are a symbol of Malaysia's economic growth and development. The towers are connected by a skybridge on the 41st and 42nd floors, offering stunning views of the city. The Petronas Twin Towers are a popular tourist destination and a iconic landmark in Kuala Lumpur.
