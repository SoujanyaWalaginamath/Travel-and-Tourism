MOUNT FUJI

Mount Fuji, located on the main island of Honshu in Japan, is the country's highest peak, standing at 3,776 meters (12,388 feet) tall. This active stratovolcano is considered one of Japan's Three Holy Mountains and is a popular destination for hiking and tourism. Its symmetrical cone, surrounded by lush forests and picturesque villages, has made it a iconic symbol of Japan and a source of inspiration for artists and writers for centuries.
